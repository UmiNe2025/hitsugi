# AR0/AR1 blind five-second test

Version: HVR-1.0

Minimum confirmatory sample: 8 first-time participants
Exploratory results with fewer than 8 participants are recorded but cannot pass a phase gate.

## Preparation

1. Use the same save fixture, viewport, brightness, browser zoom, and 5-second exposure for current and candidate.
2. Randomise A/B order per participant. Do not reveal which is new.
3. Hide screen/region names where the question tests place identity; use grayscale where the question tests shape rather than colour.
4. The facilitator reads the fixed prompt only. No hints, corrections, or follow-up examples are allowed before the first answer.
5. Record the answer verbatim, then code it after the session.

## Fixed questions

### Home

- Where are you?
- Who or what is in danger now?
- What is the main action, and can you choose something else?

Pass per participant: correctly identifies family/home, the crisis person or empty seat, and the main decision without inventing a forced action.

### Village

- Point to the giant lantern, forge, shrine, and departure gate.
- Which place would you walk to for equipment?
- What changed in the village this month?

Pass per participant: at least 3/4 landmarks by silhouette without labels and the equipment destination without facilitator guidance.

### Dungeon pair

- Are these the same place or two different places? Why?
- Point to the likely route, boundary, landmark, and return method.
- What warns you that something unusual may be nearby?

Pass per participant: different-place answer cites both landmark and route/material shape, not colour or text alone.

### Battle

- Where is the battle taking place?
- Who is acting, who are they targeting, and what is likely to happen next?
- How would you change or confirm the target?

Pass per participant: region, actor/target, next danger, and explicit confirmation path are all correct.

### Pact / Forge / Storehouse

- Where are you, what is the main subject, what can you do now, and how do you go back?
- For Pact: can you inspect this deity even if you cannot afford the contract? Why can or cannot you contract?
- For equipment: identify slot and rarity without relying on colour alone.

Pass per participant: all four orientation facts plus the screen-specific rule.

## Journey task

Fixed route: `Home → walk to Forge → depart for 螢火の窪地 → one battle → return → identify the persistent trace in Village/Home`.

Record task completion, wrong action count, blocked action, help request, elapsed time, and the participant's unprompted account of whose choice caused what and what remained.

## Phase thresholds

- AR1: candidate preferred by at least 6/8; spontaneous `AI-like/generic/assembled` comment at most 1; landmark identification at least 6/8; journey success at least 6/8; wrong irreversible action 0.
- AR2: two-region distinction at least 6/8 with landmark and route-shape explanations; region/target/next-danger answer at least 6/8.
- AR3: current place, subject, main action, and return path at least 6/8.
- AR5: a fresh sample of 8; candidate preference at least 6/8; each major cause code at most 1; journey at least 6/8; blocked action 0.

Any missing participant, changed prompt, non-random order, or facilitator hint is recorded and excluded rather than converted into a pass.
